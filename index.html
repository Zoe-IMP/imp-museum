1935 • FOUNDING & WAR EFFORT

War Production: The Ripcord Miracle
When metal shortages ended civilian production during WWII, Art pivoted to military contracts. Without blueprints or specs, he promised a parachute manufacturer 100,000 ripcord assemblies and delivered. Working nights with his son Jut, he tooled every piece himself.  By war’s end, Wasley Products had produced over two million ripcords and countless other aircraft parts. It transformed a local lighting shop into a national defense manufacturer, proof that innovation thrives under pressure. EndFragment
Toolmakers were so essential to the war effort that Jut Wasley was officially exempted from service so he could keep producing critical parts at home. EndFragment
1941–45 • FOUNDING & WAR EFFORT
Wasley Products, Inc.
Plainville Metal Works incorporates as Wasley Products, Inc., formalizing its transition to an industrial manufacturer. Art becomes President; Jut Wasley, Vice President. Together they build the foundation for modern operations. EndFragment
Art often visited the shop floor unannounced just to talk with workers. Those “walk and talks” became part of company culture for decades. EndFragment
1951 • EXPANSION & DIVERSIFICATION
1955 • EXPANSION & DIVERSIFICATION
In August 1955, the Pequabuck River rose overnight and swallowed the Farmington plant up to the second floor. Machinery, inventory, and records vanished under muddy water. Damage exceeded the company’s total worth.  Instead of giving up, every employee helped rebuild. Within months, production restarted, and by 1959 a new two-story office stood on the same site, stronger than before. The flood became a story of Wasley resilience: when the river rose, so did they. A Family Legacy Continues
1984 • INNOVATION & REINVENTION
When a friend at Zimmer-Bailey needed a surgical positioning device fast, Alan Wasley and James Bailey built it themselves. The McGuire Pelvic Positioner became the foundation of Innovative Medical Products (IMP), combining engineering precision with medical compassion. From one urgent prototype, IMP grew into a leader in orthopedic positioning, partnering with surgeons worldwide and transforming patient care. EndFragment
The first batch of IMP devices was hand-assembled using tools from the old lighting division, a literal blend of past and future. EndFragment

Disney’s “Space Mountain” Rescue
1988 • INNOVATION & REINVENTION
When Disney World’s Space Mountain shut down due to obsolete bearing seals, engineers turned to Plainville. Wasley reverse-engineered the parts within days and brought the ride back to life.  The story went national: a Connecticut manufacturer known for ripcords had saved America’s favorite roller coaster. From wartime parachutes to theme park thrills, Wasley ingenuity came full circle. EndFragment
Decades after WWII, Wasley once again supported U.S. forces, producing Teflon and Viton seals for Apache helicopters, HUMVs, and jet fighters during Operation Desert Storm.  Employees built a “Wall of Honor” in the cafeteria with photos of family serving overseas and sent care packages to troops, a continuation of the company’s wartime spirit of unity and pride.
